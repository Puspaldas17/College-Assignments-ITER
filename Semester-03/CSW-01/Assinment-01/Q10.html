S = vᵢt + ½at²: The Equation of Linear Motion

The formula S = vᵢt + ½at² describes the motion of an object under constant acceleration. As Sir Isaac Newton once said, "An object in motion stays in motion unless acted upon by an external force." This fundamental principle underlies the equation, which accurately predicts the displacement of objects.

  • S represents the displacement.
  • vᵢ is the initial velocity.
  • t is the time.
  • a is the acceleration.
  • ½at² is the additional displacement due to acceleration.

Note: This equation was originally formulated in the context of classical mechanics and has been widely used since its introduction.
